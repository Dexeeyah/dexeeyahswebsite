The 17 Sustainable Development Goals

History

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015, provides a shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet. At its heart are the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which are an urgent call for action by all countries in a global partnership.

  • 1992 – Earth Summit in Rio de Janeiro adopted Agenda 21
  • 2000 – Millennium Declaration and Millennium Development Goals (MDGs)
  • 2002 – Johannesburg Summit reaffirmed global commitments
  • 2012 – Rio+20 outcome: "The Future We Want"
  • 2013 – UN Open Working Group formed to propose SDGs
  • 2015 – Adoption of the 2030 Agenda with 17 SDGs
  • Other 2015 agreements:
    • Sendai Framework (Disaster Risk Reduction)
    • Addis Ababa Action Agenda (Finance)
    • Paris Agreement (Climate)
